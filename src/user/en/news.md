# The News portal

**News** is the reading portal for current affairs: articles, investigations, reports and press releases from professional sources (press agencies, recognised media, organisations). Accessing News requires you to be logged in.

## Tabs

The feed is organised into five tabs. The last tab you viewed is remembered and reopened on your next visit.

| Tab | Content |
|---|---|
| **All** | The full feed: every published article. |
| **Agences** (Agencies) | Articles from the press agencies you follow. |
| **Médias** (Media) | Articles from the media you follow. |
| **Journalistes** (Journalists) | Articles from the journalists you follow. |
| **Idées & Comm** | Press releases. |

The **Agences**, **Médias** and **Journalistes** tabs rely on your network: they only show publications from the sources you follow (from the **Social** portal). Until you follow someone, these tabs prompt you to follow sources.

Each tab shows the most recent publications (30 per page), newest first.

## Filtering and sorting

### Filters

Seven filters refine the feed (they apply to both articles and press releases):

- **Secteur** (Sector) — sector of activity.
- **Rubrique** (Heading).
- **Genre**.
- **Thématique** (Theme).
- **Pays**, **Département**, **Ville** (Country, Department, City) — geographic filters.

Each filter is a drop-down whose values come from the content actually published. An active filter appears as a "`filter: value`" chip that you remove with a click on its cross. You can combine several values and several filters; filter state is remembered per tab.

Clicking a **tag** on an article automatically applies a tag filter and switches to the **All** tab.

### Sorting

The **"Trier"** (Sort) menu offers: **Date** (default), **Popularity (views)**, **Popularity (likes)**, **Popularity (shares)**, **Sales**.

## The article card

In the feed, each article is shown as a card: heading, date, cover image, title, summary, author (photo, name, role and organisation), the target media where relevant, and a social bar (**Like**, **comments**, **views**).

The "views" counter (eye icon) reflects the number of **paid consultations** of the article — an indicator of the genuine interest in the content.

## The article page

The detail page shows the title, author, publication date (and last edit), an image carousel and the content.

The side column groups together:

- an **"Acquisition"** section (see [Monetisation & rights](monetisation.md)) when purchase options are available;
- a **"Métadonnées"** (Metadata) section: activity (publication and purchase dates…), author, publishing organisation, target media, then **Type**, **Genre**, **Heading**, **Subject**, **Sector** and location;
- clickable **Tags**.

### Full reading and preview

Some articles are paid. If you don't hold a reading right, the article is shown as a **truncated preview** with an invitation to acquire a consultation right. The full list of rights (consultation, gifted consultation, proof of publication, reproduction rights) is presented in [Monetisation & rights](monetisation.md).

### Reacting and commenting

- **Like**: like/unlike an article; the counter updates instantly.
- **Comments**: the zone at the bottom lists comments (newest first). Type your message and click **"Commenter"** (Comment); a confirmation is shown.

## Idées & Comm (press releases)

The **Idées & Comm** tab gathers the press releases published by organisations and their press officers. Their layout is the same as articles (title, images, content, metadata, comments), with two differences: press releases are **always freely accessible** (no Acquisition section) and benefit from the same filters as the rest of the feed.
